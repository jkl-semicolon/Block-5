Bears

  • Mona

Bears are large, robust mammals with a thick fur coat that can be black, brown, or white. They are adaptable omnivores found in diverse geographic locations across the globe.

Giraffes

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

Giraffes, native to Africa's savannas and woodlands, are the world's tallest terrestrial animals. Their long necks and unique anatomy allow them to eat leaves and shoots unreachable to other herbivores.

Giraffe Facts

Lions

  • Mella
  • Karl

Lions, predominantly found in Africa's grasslands and savannas, are considered the "king of the jungle". They are sociable mammals, typically living in groups called prides. Their efficient hunting skills and distinct mane on males make them one of the most recognizable big cats.

Lion Facts

Monkeys

  • Cookie
  • Earl
  • Banana Pudding

Monkeys, primarily inhabiting tropical rainforests, are known for their high intelligence and agility. These versatile primates, varying in size and color, have a diet consisting of fruits, leaves, and insects.

Alligators

  • Wren
  • Aspen
  • Mika

Alligators, predominantly found in freshwater environments in the Americas and China, are large reptiles recognized for their armored bodies and powerful jaws. Their diet mainly includes fish, birds, and other small mammals.
